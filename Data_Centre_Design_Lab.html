Altitude Derating — Windhoek, Namibia (1,654 m ASL)
Rule: 1% capacity derating per 100 m above 1,000 m

Derating %  = (1,654 − 1,000) ÷ 100 × 1% = 6.54%
Per unit cap. = 40.3 kW × (1 − 0.0654) = 37.7 kW
Two units    = 37.7 × 2 = 75.4 kW — covers 40.3 kW IT load ✓
UPS Heat Loss Calculation
Grid input power  = 40,317.1 W ÷ 0.96 = 41,996.9 W
UPS heat released = 41,996.9 − 40,317.1 = 1,679.8 W

Waste heat factor: (1 − 0.96) ÷ 0.96 = 0.04167 → 4.167% of IT load
Verification: 40,317.1 × 0.04167 = 1,679.8 W ✓

06 // Conclusion

Design Summary

This design specifies a Tier-1 compliant data centre housing all required equipment across 6 standard 42U racks within a 16.056 m² room. The layout accounts for active equipment, structured cabling with a 1:1 patch panel ratio, vertical cable management, horizontal patching distribution, rear-access airflow separation, and a dedicated expansion bay.

Two Magnizon MU40KXL UPS units operating in parallel provide 75.4 kW of altitude-derated capacity — derated 6.54% for Windhoek's elevation of 1,654 m ASL — comfortably covering the 40.3 kW IT load with 87% headroom. Online mode operation ensures zero transfer delay on any utility power failure.

The Vertiv Liebert CRV4 (CR066) in-row precision cooling unit handles the full 48.4 kW total heat load (IT equipment + 1,679.8 W UPS waste heat + 6,422.4 W building gain at 400 W/m²), with 16.2 kW (25.1%) of capacity headroom for peak load and future expansion.
